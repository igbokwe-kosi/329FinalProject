How to Stay Safe While Gaming

By Seth Pullishy

Do you like video games? Good. Are you ever worried about potentially giving your info to strangers, or accidentally risking your data? Also, good. Fear creates preparation and preparation is always a good thing.

So, when gaming here are the risks: doxing, leaving other data visible and data selling. First, we’ll talk about doxing, this is when someone releases private data, name, address, phone number etc., of someone online. All without the user's permission. This happens in several ways, checking the username on other websites, phishing, stalking, tracking IP addresses, packet sniffing, and data brokers. Now I’m gonna explain each of these risks. The first is when you use your username in multiple places, so they just type it in on Instagram, or discord, or whatever. Phishing is about sending you a message with some hazardous material in it (check out our malware topic!). Stalking can involve finding your information through social media and y’know doing general stalking (this is bad). Tracking IP addresses is more interesting, each computer has a unique IP address, they can use that try to discover more information. So how do they find the IP address, if you’re on an internet forum the IP address is saved with your account so the moderators can see who you really are, so a hacker could potentially get it from there. If you’ve left a comment they could get it through that as well. This is not to say that it is EASY, but it is indeed possible. I actually know people who personally were doxed when playing games, and it truly is scary (for them). Some of these involve common sense, some may involve the police and some certainly will involve VPN’s to solve (in the order laid out above).

Continuing into packet sniffing, this is effectively just when a hacker intercepts your internet data through some means. Once they have that data, they can try looking for anything you send over the internet, texts, banking info, etc. This can be combatted effectively with a VPN as it will encrypt your data so they cannot read it.

Finally, is a data broker: a company that sells data. All websites with cookies keep information about you, because it is incredibly useful for them and convenient for you. It remembers who you are, so you don’t always have to sign in, or keeps stuff in your cart, etc. However, some companies are more than happy to sell your data for money to anyone who will buy it. So, if they sell it, why couldn’t some weirdo buy it right? Data brokers are a serious issue and the best solution to them is probably legislative, that way companies cannot legally sell your data away. I believe the US tends to have looser laws than most so if you’re an American please be careful!

The last bit I wanna mention is the history of doxing. It has been around since the 90’s, before the internet and was used to reveal information about some hackers. It became very well known over a decade ago when many KKK members were doxed. As well, the murderer of Cecil the Lion was doxed and when the Boston Marathon Bombing the internet tried to find the bombers, but just led to innocent people tragically being harassed.
