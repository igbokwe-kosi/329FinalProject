By Seth Pullishy

HEY!! Welcome back, I can see you’re getting addicted to my wit and sense of humour. Anyway, VPN time! So, we’re gonna talk about what it is, the science behind it and some really good ones you can pay for!

Ok VPN stands for Virtual Private Network, which allows you to create your own, non-physical secure network instead of using potentially (and probably) unsafe ones! What separates virtual and physical ones includes the lack of a physical body and how it is protecting your data. Physically speaking, it doesn’t have routers, and modems, and cables, and wires and hardware. And for protection instead of using firewalls and access controls it uses encryption.

So, here’s a simple deep dive on encryption; first we need to understand 2 types of messages and how we encrypt one. Imagine you are passing messages between you and your friend Bob in class, but some punk named Eve keeps reading them. The plaintext is when you write “Hi Bob” and Eve can read “Hi Bob”. To stop this we convert out message to ciphertext, which is when you write “Hi Bob” and Eve can read “NOKW S” (enigma code). When Bob receives the message, he will know how to decrypt it back into “Hi Bob”. To encrypt we take the letters given to use and we change them up, and swap letters around and change the order of the alphabet, etc.

It’s not truly important to understand how a VPN will encrypt the algorithm and change up our 1’s and 0’s, but it is important to understand why. We want to stop Eve from reading our data so we encrypt it and give Bob the key to unlock it that way while it is in transit no one else can figure it out.

What are the risks of not using one, as getting one costs money, we need a good reason to do it. Well for one, if you use unsecured online banking then the bad guys could potentially read your data transfers which is bad. Another one is IP address finding, this is where an attacker could potentially expose where you are when they steal the data. It can also release other sensitive information, so just imagine the worst thing that is saved on your phone and now imagine some skeevy pervert getting it.
