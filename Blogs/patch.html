Why Should You Patch?

By Seth Pullishy

Y’know how every morning your computer asks to update, and you say remind me tomorrow? This is why you should stop. We’re gonna tell you what a patch is, why they are important and what the risks of not updating could be.

So, to start off, what is a patch? A patch is a kind of update that repairs a security flaw, imagine you own a chicken coop and there’s a hole in the side so now coyotes can get in. Well, you would naturally patch that hole with something. It is important to note that it’s not like fixing that hole and making it brand new, sometimes it can cause other issues, but it surely stops the attackers from using a guaranteed security flaw.

So, now that we know what it is and why it could be important, where do we find out where to do it. Well, Windows and Mac will give you an update prompt and some of the more popular Linux’s even automatically update for you so that’s simple. Most software I have ever used also gives a prompt or doesn’t let you use it until there’s an update so that is convenient!

Almost finally, what are the risks of not updating? Well, time for another strange analogy! Imagine your computer is a castle, and your moat guy keeps telling you it needs an update, the crocodiles are old, the edges aren’t steep enough it is a risk! But you ignore him, and then one day the enemy comes. They reach the walls and can cross the simple moat and come in and kill you. Well, that is what we want to prevent, we don’t want cyberattacks of any kind, and leaving any vulnerabilities means these attacks become more likely. People could try and attack the same system flaw for months or years so over time you are more likely to get attacked.

Finally, I wanna tell you what EOL software is, EOL stands for end-of-life, and it simply means that the developers are done with it. They aren’t gonna update it anymore, which means if a flaw is discovered (which over time becomes more and more likely), there will be no one to fix it. Our very last info citation is a site where you can search to see if any of your software has EOL’d.

Be safe, and make sure you upgrade your moat as much as possible, insert cheesy pun here.
