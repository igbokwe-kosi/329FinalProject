Top 10 Ways to Stay Safe

By Seth Pullishy

The internet is a fun place, but also really scary in other parts (terrorists have webpages for example), and those scary places can colour your interpretation of the rest of the web. So, we wanted to give you a list of 10 things you can do to protect yourself, your computer, your data and your loved ones. Note: this list is not in any particular order.

10. Never use personal information

This is a big one when interacting with people. It’s recommended that you avoid using usernames or passwords that identify you. We don’t wanna use a username that is just your name, we don’t want to be tied to anything online. Obviously if you have Instagram for example, you probably use your name and have photos of yourself, but you also probably only communicate with friends and family. However, on sites like Reddit, YouTube or your favourite cooking blog you probably don’t need to be identifiable. Most of the people you interact with don’t know and shouldn’t know you. To stay protected it is recommended that you also never share that information. Don’t tell anyone where you live, or who you are. This is because you don’t know them, and they could be a bad actor. To stay safe, your real life and virtual identities should be distinct, so that people cannot look at your name or upload history and figure out who you are.

9. Stay mainstream

Do you know where the best place to hide a body is? The second page of Google. No one has ever looked there. The internet is VAST, so much so that Google is ONLY 5%!!!! That other 95% is like the jungles of South America, largely unexplored and probably unforgiving, so why go there? It is best to stay in trustworthy sites, the high traffic ones, not because they are perfect, but because we know who owns them and who runs them. There is really no need to dive into the depths, and if you do you just run the risk of getting more malware. The photo is Bad Bunny I believe, but showed up on a stock footage site when I typed dark web and I think he looks cool.

8. Stay organized

By this, I mean account organization. The average person has 240 accounts with a password (according to Dashlane.com) and that amount is surely unmanageable but there are programs you can use to help. We certainly recommend a password manager, and if you have a Google or Gmail account you have one built in. These services will remember your username and password so that you don’t have to. By keeping yourself organized you will hopefully be able to keep stronger passwords instead of the same password 240 times, and the more you have the safer you are.

7. Two-Factor Authentication

Two-factor authentication refers to having to use two pieces of ID to sign-in. Most commonly it is a username/password and an SMS text message. This is better because an attacker needs to work harder to get the second piece, but certainly not perfect. These messages can be intercepted and used by an attacker (explained in the hackcontrol.org link), but it is more work for an attacker and therefore better. It is important to not get a false sense of security though, and to stay on guard.

6. Avoid strangers

Stranger danger is still an important phrase these days and maybe even more important in the modern era. You don’t know who that person is, so it may be best to avoid them, and please, PLEASE do not give them personal info. A stranger could dox you, which is when a stranger posts private information about you like an IP address or where you live, which can be done through hacking so try not to engage with individuals you don’t know when possible.

5. Strong passwords

Passwords! So vital. In this case we’re just gonna refer you to how to create a strong password (also check out our password game), but we are gonna tell you why. If you have a weak password, somebody could sign in as you and pretend to be you! That’s bad! By keeping a strong password, you can keep a strong lock on the gate to your digital identity.

4. Stay updated

This means software updating, when a company releases a patch, it could have very vital security features being updated because a leak was found. If you don’t update it, then you are potentially leaving yourself vulnerable to bad actors. We recommend updating all of your software immediately. The most important being your OS, in the Windows update or whatever Apple uses to update (Windows superiority gang).

3. Keep family safe

We want to remind you to tell your loved ones how to stay safe. All of these apply to them and doing this could also keep you safe. If a loved one gets hacked, then the attacker may use that trusted account to send you malware to compromise your account. This happened to me, buddy’s Instagram was hacked, and the hacker tried to get me to sign up for a gift card or something. So, if he was able to keep that hacker away then I would never have been given the chance to be compromised. Keep your people safe!

2. Don’t click on popups (usually)

If you get a weird email with a link don’t click on it. If you’re on a website and you get a sudden pop –up that says “HACKERS ARE CLOSING IN DOWNLOAD THIS TO STOP THEM” don’t click on it. Also never download something from an untrusted source, which to be fair, is most of them. Popups can give you a virus by downloading them so don’t do it!

1. Get a VPN

This one is big, and we are gonna go into depth on VPNs on the page labeled “What IS a VPN?”. The main purpose of this is to mask your identity so that no other individual can see who you are. In my opinion, the most important point to understand here is how it prevents those doxing attacks from earlier (when someone leaks information about you like an IP address). It masks your IP address so an attacker cannot see it, and that is a great step to preserving your identity.
