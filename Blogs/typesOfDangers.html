Virus

A computer virus is the most common type of malware most people get. Computer viruses get hold of clean files and from there corrupts them and corrupts any other files that come in touch with them as well. Computer viruses do this by making copies of themselves and sticking them onto different computer programs and files. Once someone opens one of these infected files it spreads throughout your computer which creates problems as it can steal your personal information and stop your laptop from performing multiple everyday tasks making the computer useless. Think of a computer virus like a cold your computer can catch, just like how humans catch a cold they can spread their cold to others by sneezing or getting in close proximity of the infected human. Similar to computer virus it spreads itself by implementing and inserting code onto your computer files which damages your laptop. To help identify a computer virus can be easy if you're careful when browsing the web or downloading or opening softwares, just like you can be when you're out and about in the world you're cautious and careful. Some way to identify them is by not downloading just any software or opening and downloading files from an email to see if the sender of the email or the software you're downloading is from a person you trust and know. Websites that seem sketchy or have a bunch of pop ads that redirect you to different pages are also red flags and should avoid them as they can lead to a virus. Read reviews or videos to see what other people say and their experiences when downloading apps/programs. If something online seems too good to be true like free money by watching a video/ad or free movies or streams it's most likely a scam that will lead to a virus on your computer. Ways to prevent your computer from getting a virus is by downloading/installing antivirus tools such as Mcafee and keeping them updated at all times, run antivirus scans, don't use unsecure networks, think twice before clicking, create strong and secure passwords and also use a firewall.

Worms

A computer worm is like a virus they can corrupt and kill your computer memory or storage. A difference between the two is that worms can spread by themselves, they don't need to attach themselves to files or programs on your computer to spread. Worms make duplicates of themselves and spread instantly and quickly across many devices. To help avoid and prevent yourself from getting a computer worm you must first be able to identify how they look and the different ways/forms they come in. One way is by being careful with email and links and files that come with them only open or download the ones you believe are safe by knowing the person who sent you the email etc. Don't click on random ads that seem too good to be true, only download stuff that are from credible sources, download antivirus tool and have them updated at all time, and again create strong passwords have a firewall ready on your computer, don't just connect to any free WI-Fi and be careful when connecting to such, regularly backup your files and pictures as well in case of a attack. By implementing these steps and being cautious online it makes it harder for attackers to attack you and your computer.

Trojan horses

A trojan horse virus is like the same concept and attack method as the real trojan horse attack, Trojan horse virus can be seen and shown to a user as something they like or need. It may be appealing to them but in reality it's not something they expected because of this it will lead them into downloading certain stuff causing catastrophic damages to their computer. Trojan viruses can be usually found in emails or pop ups on sketchy websites, they often come as antivirus programs and pretend like they are real and legit and will help keep your computer safe but in reality it will cause much damage. To help spot these trojans one can avoid downloading sketchy software and clicking on links on email sent from individuals you don't know or can identify. Another way to avoid getting a trojan virus is by again downloading firewalls and antivirus software and making sure to always keep them updated. Following these steps can help avoid trojan virus and many other virus types as well.

Ransomware

Ransomware malware is a virus that acts like a virtual kidnapper, they take and lock your files and important information and to get such files back you would need to pay money or some sort of payment to get those files back. A way to identify this is if a message comes up on your screen asking for money is a ransomware attack, in this type of case unplug your computer and seek professional help. If you fall victim to this scam do not pay the attackers as it can lead to much more problems as they ask for personal information, card details, etc. Ways to prevent and protect yourself from a ransomware attack is by being careful and fully alert when opening and downloading content from emails. Downloading antivirus softwares and keeping them up to date. Keeping your system up to date. Backup important files. Taking these steps minimizes the chances of a ransomware attack and even if such an attack still happens your files are backed up.

Spyware

A spyware malware is something that steals your personal information without your knowledge or consent. This virus can also allow the attackers to watch over what you do on your computer at all times. The attackers can record and listen to what passwords you enter, they can also see what keys are being pressed on your keyboard, what websites and videos are being looked at. Scary part about this malware is that you are completely unaware someone is watching what you do which can be very dangerous for your safety. Ways to identify you have been attacked by a spyware malware is by noticing if your computer has been running slower than it used to be, if you start noticing a bunch of ads, check if there has been charges to your bank account that you don't recognize. Ways to prevent this malware on your computer is by running anti virus scans on your computer regularly and updating your computer software regularly as well.

Adware

Adware malware is similar to spyware but causes less harm. What it does is it spams and fills your entire screen with unwanted ads. Adware also monitors your activity online, what you like and preferences. It may not seem harmful but it's still an invasion of privacy and can maybe down the road cause security/safety problems. To avoid this attack you can download an ad blocker to block these ads. It can also be fixed by a good security/antivirus software.

Rootkits

A rootkit malware is a devious program that allows an attacker to have full control over your computer without your knowledge/consent. Rootkits can let the attackers gain full access, grant them full access to admin controls and they can also spy/monitor your activity. These attacks are hard to spot and if infected by one will lead to you either buying a new laptop or wiping your entire computer to get rid of them. Rootkit malware are usually conducted by the attackers sending out emails and sketchy downloads. Ways to prevent these attacks is by opening or downloading content that you trust and/or recognize, downloading anti virus systems and keeping your devices up to date.

Keyloggers

Keylogger, also called keystroke logging, is a malware that secretly records every button you press on your keyboard. This is a dangerous malware as it can get access to your password for your bank accounts. This kind of malware is tricky and hard to detect by the human eye. A special tool is needed to detect it. A way to detect a keylogger malware is by downloading security softwares they can easily detect it. The only way you can notice this malware is by noticing if your computer has slowed down or not. Another way to protect yourself is by keeping the software up to date using strong and unique passwords and also enabling two factor authentications as well.

Fileless malware

A fileless malware is a malware that hides in your computer memory and exploits programs on your computer to do malicious activity, this malware also disrupts anti virus softwares and steals all/any kinds of data. This kind of virus is very hard to detect and see with the human eye as it's a unique type of malware that doesn't require to be put on your computer by downloads or opening links. For this kind of malware special tools are the only way to detect and get rid of these viruses. There are many ways to protect yourself, one of them being keeping all your devices software up to date, limit the ability of opening any and all programs have restrictions and control on what can be opened and what should not be open. Buy and install more secure and advanced security measures to eliminate any opportunities and potential risk/attack from happening.

Botnets

A bot or botnet virus malware is multiple corrupted laptops all controlled by an attacker. This attack can slow down your computer as they use and consume all the resources on your computer. They can do this by using your computers to perform more harmful tasks such as sending out spam emails and other kinds of malicious acts. This malware can come in contact on someone's computer by them opening up links sent via emails or downloading sketchy softwares. To prevent and keep yourself safe from this malware always have your system software up to date, instal and have antivirus and firewalls active they can scan for these threats and protect you. Open links and download programs you trust and can identify. If you notice that your computer is slow or tends to crash a lot seek professional guidance to help fix and stay safe from this malware.
